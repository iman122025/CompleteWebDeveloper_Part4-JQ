Intellectual and philosophical books

Being and Nothingness

Being and Nothingness is the major philosophical work of Jean-Paul Sartre, a central figure in existentialist thought. In this dense and challenging book, Sartre explores the nature of human existence through the lens of phenomenology, influenced by Edmund Husserl and Martin Heidegger.

A Brief History of Time

A Brief History of Time is a groundbreaking science book by physicist Stephen Hawking, aimed at helping non-scientific readers understand complex topics in cosmology and the nature of the universe.

Sophie's World

Sophie's World is a unique novel that serves as both a mystery story and an introduction to the history of Western philosophy. Written by Norwegian author Jostein Gaarder, the book follows the story of Sophie Amundsen, a 14-year-old girl living in Norway who suddenly starts receiving mysterious letters

How to Stop Worrying and Start Living

How to Stop Worrying and Start Living is a classic self-help book by Dale Carnegie, best known for his earlier bestseller How to Win Friends and Influence People. This book is designed to help readers overcome worry, stress, and anxiety in everyday life.
